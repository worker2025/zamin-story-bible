# Film Bible: General Continuity Rules

## Rule 1 — Source hierarchy
For Phase 2 production documentation, the Story Bible (V00-V10) is the only
canonical source. The manuscript itself is not consulted in this phase.
Any apparent gap in the Story Bible is treated as UNKNOWN, not as an
invitation to re-derive detail from the novel.

## Rule 2 — No invented connective tissue
Where the Story Bible does not document a transition between two scenes
(e.g., how a character physically traveled between two locations), no
transition is invented. Production teams must treat such gaps as open
craft decisions outside canon.

## Rule 3 — Unresolved plot threads remain unresolved
The following are explicitly UNKNOWN in the Story Bible and must not be
resolved in any derivative production material without being flagged as
non-canonical invention:
- Hani's fate after departing the palace (Chapter 9)
- Halazh's fate after his arrest (Chapter 8)
- Nisiker's fate after relinquishing the treasury seal (Chapter 9)
- The army commander's fate after defection (Chapters 7-9)
- The identity of Nika's killer (Chapter 7)
- The identity of Kuru's killer (Chapter 13)

## Rule 4 — Historicity distinction preserved
Only Hani, Hohin, and Shoterouro are documented as historically-grounded
characters; only Ayapir, Kolfarah, Khong-e-Azhdar, and Eshkaft-e-Salman are
documented as real, historically attested locations (Source: V01_World.md).
This distinction must be preserved in any production framing (e.g.,
marketing material distinguishing "based on a real figure" claims).

## Rule 5 — Symbol/typography conventions
The manuscript's own structural markers (blank line = time/place passage;
dash = scene change within chapter; asterisk = new section; triple
asterisk = end of chapter) are documented in V01_World.md and may inform
scene-break pacing decisions, but are not prescriptive shot-length rules.

## Validation
- No invented information
- Derived only from Story Bible
- UNKNOWN preserved
- Canon preserved
