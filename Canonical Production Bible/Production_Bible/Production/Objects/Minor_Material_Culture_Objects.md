# Object Sheet: Minor Material-Culture Objects

## Overview
This composite sheet lists smaller objects/terms defined by footnotes in the Story Bible, each with only its stated definition, source context, and no invented elaboration.

### پشموخُمه (Pashm-o-khomeh)
- Material: spun wool thread/yarn hanks, used for weaving/carpet-work.
- Owner: general/communal (part of the levy demanded from Kohbad households).
- Appearance: Chapter 1, tax-levy dialogue.
- Meaning: UNKNOWN beyond practical use.
(Source: V05_Objects.md)

### تَهْدِه (Tahdeh)
- Material: wood.
- Description: a type of wooden cradle for infants.
- Owner: general/communal.
- Meaning: UNKNOWN beyond practical use.
(Source: V05_Objects.md)

### مَالر (Malar)
- Material: wood (three-legged/tripod construction).
- Description: used to hang and churn a doogh-skin.
- Owner: general/communal.
- Meaning: UNKNOWN beyond practical use.
(Source: V05_Objects.md)

### بَرْد و کُچِک (Berd o kuchek)
- Material: stone.
- Description: various types of small, throwable stones.
- Owner: general/communal (used in the Kolfarah rescue, Chapter 2).
- Meaning: UNKNOWN beyond practical/weapon use in that scene.
(Source: V05_Objects.md)

### َپهْرِه (Pahreh)
- Material: wood or iron.
- Description: a spindle, a simple tool for spinning cotton or wool.
- Owner: general/communal; associated with an unnamed spinning woman at Golshor.
- Meaning: UNKNOWN beyond practical use.
(Source: V05_Objects.md, V08_Professions.md)

### دستاس (Dastas — hand-mill)
- Material: stone (two flat stones).
- Description: used by women to grind grain by hand at Golshor.
- Owner: general/communal.
- Meaning: UNKNOWN beyond practical use. Raju notes an alternative water-driven technology exists in Sind.
(Source: V05_Objects.md)
