# Style Guide: Production Rules

## Purpose
This guide converts Story Bible constraints into explicit production
prohibitions and permissions. Each rule is traceable to a Story Bible file.
Where a prohibition is not directly derivable from the Story Bible but is
a reasonable production-safety inference from the documented setting (late
Neo-Elamite period, per V01_World.md), it is marked as such and separated
from directly sourced rules.

## Rules Directly Derived from Story Bible

1. Respect the stated setting: late Neo-Elamite period, Zagros region,
   approximately 2700 years before the author's writing (Source:
   V01_World.md — "حدود ۲۷۰۰ سال پیش").
2. Respect the stated historicity split: only Hani, Hohin, and Shoterouro
   are historically-grounded characters; only Ayapir, Kolfarah,
   Khong-e-Azhdar, and Eshkaft-e-Salman are real, historically attested
   sites (Source: V01_World.md).
3. Respect documented architecture materials only: stone and clay/mud
   construction, as documented per location (Source: V04_Locations.md,
   Visual/Architecture.md). Do not introduce construction materials or
   methods not documented.
4. Respect documented weapon/tool inventory: spears, shields, swords,
   helmets, armor for soldiers; spades, sickles, adzes, wooden clubs,
   shepherd's poles for villagers/rebels (Source: V10_VisualDesign.md).
   Do not introduce weapon types not documented.
5. Respect the documented pantheon exactly as listed in V09_Religion.md.
   Do not introduce deities, symbols, or iconography not documented there.
6. Respect the documented shura/vote-brick system exactly as described in
   V07_Culture.md and Objects/Vote-Bricks.md. Do not alter its stated
   mechanics (anonymity, brick-based, two-symbol system).
7. Do not resolve any of the six explicitly UNKNOWN plot threads listed in
   Film/General_Continuity_Rules.md, Rule 3.

## Rules Inferred as Reasonable Production-Safety Boundaries (not directly stated in Story Bible, but consistent with the documented setting and explicitly flagged as such)

- No fantasy elements (magic, mythical creatures) — the Story Bible
  documents no such elements anywhere in the novel's canon.
- No medieval European castle architecture — the Story Bible documents
  only stone/clay Near Eastern construction consistent with the stated
  Neo-Elamite setting.
- No modern clothing or fabrics — the Story Bible documents only
  leather, wool, and cotton-cloth materials.
- No Hollywood-style stylized armor (e.g., anachronistic plate armor) —
  the Story Bible documents only "زره" (armor) and "کالهخود" (helmet)
  without further design specification; any specific armor design must
  stay within a period-plausible register consistent with the documented
  Neo-Elamite/Zagros setting, and should defer to the Appendix's relief
  descriptions (round Elamite cap, etc.) where available.
- No glowing or supernatural visual effects — the Story Bible documents
  no such phenomena; the closest instance (wind moving the chang's
  strings unassisted, Chapter 10) is described as a natural/wind-caused
  event witnessed and interpreted by characters as a "sign," not as a
  depicted supernatural effect.

## Non-Canonical Additions Must Be Labeled
Any production choice filling a Story-Bible UNKNOWN (a character's face,
a costume color, a building's height, etc.) must be labeled in delivery
materials as "non-canonical / production design choice" and must not be
represented as sourced from the novel or the Story Bible.

## Validation
- No invented information
- Derived only from Story Bible
- UNKNOWN preserved
- Canon preserved
