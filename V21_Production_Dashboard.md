# V21 — Production Dashboard

This document is the operational control center for the entire project —
Master Asset Database, Character Tracker, Location Tracker, Object
Tracker, Image Database, Prompt Database, Shot Database, Storyboard
Tracker, Video Tracker, Continuity Control, Quality Assurance, Version
Control, Naming Standards, Production Pipeline, Milestones, Production
Statistics, and Change Log. It is not a Story Bible and not a Production
Bible. It contains no canon. Every production asset — every image,
concept art piece, storyboard, shot, video, revision, and approval — must
be registered here as it is produced. As of this document's creation, no
images, shots, storyboards, or videos have been produced; all trackers
below reflect that starting state and are schemas ready to be filled in
as work proceeds.

This document was renamed from V21_Asset_Tracker.md to
V21_Production_Dashboard.md to reflect its full scope as the project's
sole production management tool. Its structure is now FROZEN: no further
redesign, reorganization, or duplicate management documents. Only its
content (table rows, statistics, change log entries) is updated as
production proceeds.

Asset IDs for Characters, Locations, and Objects reuse the IDs already
assigned in Production/Characters/, Production/Locations/, and
Production/Objects/. New ID ranges are assigned in this document for
asset types that did not previously have individual tracked IDs
(Architecture, Plant, Animal, Food, Material), following the same
convention, and applied only to entities already named in the Story
Bible / Production Bible — no new entities are introduced.

# Section 13 — Version Control

```
Draft -> v0.1 -> v0.2 -> v1.0 -> v1.1 -> ... -> Locked
                                                    |
                                                    v
                                               Deprecated
                                                    |
                                                    v
                                               Archived
```

- `Draft` — work in progress, not yet submitted for review.
- `v0.1`, `v0.2` — early internal iterations, pre-review.
- `v1.0` — first version submitted for full Quality Assurance pass
  (Section 12).
- `v1.1` and further minor versions — revisions requested during QA.
- `Locked` — approved, production-ready, frozen version. Not edited in
  place; further change requires a new version number.
- `Deprecated` — a formerly Locked version superseded by a later Locked
  version. Retained in the Image/Video Database for traceability.
- `Archived` — retained for historical record only; not used in active
  production and not surfaced in default tracker views.

# Section 15 — File Naming Standard

Format: `{AssetID}_{AssetName}_{Descriptor}_v{VersionNumber}.{ext}`

Examples:
- `CHAR-001_Nika_Front_v001.png`
- `CHAR-001_Nika_ProfileLeft_v001.png`
- `CHAR-001_Nika_CloseUp_v002.png`
- `LOC-002_Kolfarah_Dawn_v001.png`
- `OBJ-003_Chang_CloseUp_v001.png`
- `SHOT-021_v003.png`
- `VID-005_SC021_v002.mp4`

Descriptors must correspond to a documented pose, angle, time-of-day, or
condition (per Sections 3, 4, and 5 of this tracker, and the underlying
Production Bible sheets). A descriptor with no canonical basis (e.g.,
"Front" for a character whose face is UNKNOWN) may still be produced as a
necessary production choice, but the corresponding Image Database row
(Section 6) must note it as a non-canonical production design choice in
its Comments field.

# Appendix — Future AI Production Workflow

This appendix is informative only. It does not describe canon. It
documents the intended production workflow for the remainder of the
project, building on the pipeline already summarized in Section 16.

## Stage 1 — Story_Bible

- Purpose: Preserve the manuscript's canonical facts, with full
  Chapter/Quote evidence, under a Zero Invention Policy.
- Primary Inputs: The manuscript ("زمین خواهد شنید").
- Primary Outputs: V00_README_Index.md through V10_VisualDesign.md.
- Responsible System: Canon Repository.

## Stage 2 — Production_Bible

- Purpose: Reorganize Story Bible facts into production-oriented sheets
  (Characters, Locations, Objects, Visual, Film, AI, Prompts, Style
  Guide) without introducing new facts.
- Primary Inputs: Story_Bible (V00-V10).
- Primary Outputs: Production/ folder (Characters/, Locations/,
  Objects/, Shots/, Visual/, Film/, AI/, Prompts/, Style_Guide.md).
- Responsible System: Canon Organization.

## Stage 3 — Production_Dashboard

- Purpose: Track production status, dependencies, approvals, and
  versioning for every asset identified in the Production Bible; serve as
  the operational control center.
- Primary Inputs: Production_Bible, V19_Asset_Production_Plan.md.
- Primary Outputs: This document (V21_Production_Dashboard.md), kept
  continuously updated.
- Responsible System: Production Management.

## Stage 4 — Visual Canon

- Purpose: Establish the locked creative-direction decisions (character
  designs, location designs, object designs) that fill the UNKNOWN gaps
  left by the Story Bible, explicitly flagged as non-canonical production
  choices per Production/Style_Guide.md.
- Primary Inputs: Production_Dashboard (Character/Location/Object
  Trackers, Sections 3-5), Production/Visual/Visual_Rules.md.
- Primary Outputs: Approved character/location/object design references
  (not yet produced as of this version).
- Responsible System: Creative Direction.

## Stage 5 — Reference Images

- Purpose: Generate the front/profile/back/full-body/close-up and
  documented-action-pose image sets defined in
  V19_Asset_Production_Plan.md Sections 4-6.
- Primary Inputs: Visual Canon decisions, Production/Prompts/ templates.
- Primary Outputs: Populated rows in Section 6 (Image Database).
- Responsible System: AI Image Pipeline.

## Stage 6 — Character Lock

- Purpose: Finalize and freeze each character's face, body, hair,
  costume, and props per the Character Tracker (Section 3) columns.
- Primary Inputs: Reference Images (character subset).
- Primary Outputs: Section 3 rows with Approved = Yes and Status =
  Locked.
- Responsible System: Production Management.

## Stage 7 — Location Lock

- Purpose: Finalize and freeze each location's environment and lighting
  variants per the Location Tracker (Section 4) columns.
- Primary Inputs: Reference Images (location subset).
- Primary Outputs: Section 4 rows with Approved = Yes and Status =
  Locked.
- Responsible System: Production Management.

## Stage 8 — Object Lock

- Purpose: Finalize and freeze each object's material, scale, and
  condition per the Object Tracker (Section 5) columns.
- Primary Inputs: Reference Images (object subset).
- Primary Outputs: Section 5 rows with Approved = Yes and Status =
  Locked.
- Responsible System: Production Management.

## Stage 9 — Storyboard

- Purpose: Compose scene-by-scene panels from locked Character/Location/
  Object assets, indexed to Production/Shots/Scene_Index.md.
- Primary Inputs: Character Lock, Location Lock, Object Lock outputs.
- Primary Outputs: Populated Section 9 (Storyboard Tracker), SB-001 to
  SB-013.
- Responsible System: Cinematic Planning.

## Stage 10 — Shot Design

- Purpose: Define framing, lens, camera height, movement, and
  composition for each shot, per Production/Film/ continuity files and
  any future camera documentation.
- Primary Inputs: Approved Storyboards.
- Primary Outputs: Populated Section 8 (Shot Database), SHOT-001 to
  SHOT-077.
- Responsible System: Cinematic Direction.

## Stage 11 — AI Image Generation

- Purpose: Produce final per-shot images from locked assets and approved
  shot designs.
- Primary Inputs: Shot Design outputs, Production/Prompts/ templates,
  Section 7 (Prompt Database).
- Primary Outputs: Further populated Section 6 (Image Database) entries
  tied to specific Shot IDs.
- Responsible System: Image Models.

## Stage 12 — AI Video Generation

- Purpose: Produce video clips from approved per-shot images.
- Primary Inputs: Approved Shot images (Section 6, filtered by Shot ID).
- Primary Outputs: Populated Section 10 (Video Tracker).
- Responsible System: Video Models.

## Stage 13 — Editing

- Purpose: Assemble, sequence, and refine generated video clips into
  coherent cuts.
- Primary Inputs: Approved videos (Section 10).
- Primary Outputs: Edited sequences (tracked as new Video Tracker entries
  or a dedicated edit decision list, not yet defined as of this version).
- Responsible System: Human Review.

## Stage 14 — Trailer

  sequences.
- Primary Inputs: Editing outputs.
- Primary Outputs: A Video Tracker entry marked as the trailer cut (per
  Section 18, Milestone Phase E).
- Responsible System: Marketing Production.

## Stage 15 — Feature Film

  approved edited sequences, covering all 77 scenes.
- Primary Inputs: Editing outputs (full scene coverage).
- Primary Outputs: Final feature film (per Section 18, Milestone Phase
  F: all Shot Database rows Video Ready = Yes and Approved = Yes).
- Responsible System: Final Production.
