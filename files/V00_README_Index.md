## Canon Rule Applied

Every fact in every file carries an **Evidence** trail (Chapter, and a direct
**Quote** from the manuscript in the original Persian). Where the manuscript
does not state something, the field is marked **UNKNOWN** rather than
inferred, estimated, or invented — including for major characters' ages,
appearances, and the ultimate fates of Hani, Halazh, Nisiker, and the
identities of the two unseen archers who kill Nika (Ch.7) and Kuru (Ch.13).

## Important Distinction Preserved Throughout

Per the author's own **یادداشت نویسنده** (Appendix): only **Hani, Hohin, and
Shoterouro** are stated to be historically-grounded figures, and only
**Ayapir, Kolfarah, Khong-e-Azhdar, and Eshkaft-e-Salman** are stated to be
real, historically attested sites. All other characters, events, dialogue,
and rituals are the author's explicit literary invention — but are still
recorded here as **in-fiction canon fact** of the novel itself, since this
bible's job is to preserve the novel's world, not to adjudicate real-world
history.

## Known Gaps for a Future Extraction Pass

- Some very minor unnamed background speakers (single-line dialogue at large
  crowd scenes, e.g. at Khong-e-Azhdar and Eshkaft-e-Salman) were not
  individually catalogued as characters, since the text itself does not
  name or distinguish them beyond their single line.
- Footnoted single-word glosses that are purely linguistic (e.g. isolated
  place-name etymologies not tied to any scene) were folded into the
  relevant Locations/Objects entries rather than given a separate glossary
  file; a dedicated V11_Glossary could be built on request if useful.
