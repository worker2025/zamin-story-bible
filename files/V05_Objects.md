# V05 — Objects

## چنگ (Chang) — Nika's harp

The central recurring object/symbol of the novel.

- **First introduction:** Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "چنگ را از روی طاقچه برداشت و کنار پنجره ایستاد."
- **Used to play for Manzat at Kolfarah**, described as a simple, light melody "شبیه صدای زنگولههایی که در بادِ کوهستان گم میشدند." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Played in the temple of Narsina**, prompting the confrontation with Hani. Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Set on the ground when Nika is arrested**, later returned to her by Manzat via the young soldier: "فکر کردم اگر چنگت بسوزد، آوازهایت هم میسوزد." Nika answers: "آواز توی چوب زندگی نمیکند... شاید توی آدمها." Evidence — Chapter 2.
- **Dropped when Nika is first struck during the Shileyti raid**, making "صدای بم و کوتاهی شبیه آه" (a low, brief sound like a sigh) as it hits a rock. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Nika's dying instruction:** "چنگم را... بده مانْزات. بگو... هر وقت قایقش راهش را گم کرد، چنگ بزند. صدا... راه را پیدا میکند." Nika's last physical act is to touch the strings once more with bloodied fingers. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **After death:** Ani takes the chang; it is left silent by the grave; Manzat declares "دیگر نمیزنیم. تا وقتی که نیکا برگردد," but secretly plays a single note alone at night: "ببین... هنوز اینجاست." Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **First public playing after Nika's death:** by Ani, unprompted, once rebuilding begins. Evidence — Chapter 9.
- **At Khong-e-Azhdar:** wind moves the strings on its own before anyone touches them, taken as a "sign"/"greeting." Evidence — Chapter 10.
- **At Eshkaft-e-Salman:** an unnamed teenage girl plays it for the first time (after Nika), initially clumsily, gaining confidence via a lullaby she knows; afterward it is renamed in common speech: "دیگر هیچکس نگفت چنگِ نیکاست... میگفتند: چنگِ میدان است." Evidence — Chapter 12.

## قایقِ برگ بلوط (Oak-leaf boat) — Manzat's motif object

- **First made by Manzat** at Kolfarah, floated on the pool by the temple: "قایق را روی آب رها کرد. چند وجب جلو رفت و به سنگی گیر کرد." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Becomes a copied motif among children after Nika's imprisonment/escape:** "اول مانْزات ساخت. بعد بچههای دیگر هم ساختند. حاال هر جا بروی، یکی از آنها پیدایش میشود." Evidence — Chapter 5.
- **Lost and recovered during the mountain flight** (Ch.5): Moraad suggests leaving it, Nika insists on retrieving it: "بعضی چیزها را اگر جا بگذاری، دیگر همان نیستند."
- **Nika's grave marker** is a broken brick she had drawn a boat (along with water and a sun) on, not this specific leaf-boat, but the same recurring image. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Continues as Manzat's habitual gesture** through Chapters 8–13, floated at moments of transition or grief (e.g., set adrift beside Kuru's memorial brick at the very end). Evidence — Chapter 13 — Quote: "مانْزات خشت شکسته را در جوی آب گذاشت. آب آن را آرام با خود کشاند. قایق برگ بلوط هنوز چند قدم آنطرفتر شناور بود."

## لوح (گِلی) (Clay tablet)

- The primary medium of record-keeping/writing throughout — used by Shoterouro (official records, journal entries), Hohin (private names of widows/orphans), Kuru, cataloguing tax debts (Hotran), and eventually as anonymous "vote bricks" (خشتهای رأی) in the popular council process.
- **Notable individual tablets:**
  - The Shileyti death-tally tablet Hani breaks: Chapter 2.
  - Hani's father's old tablet about the three-day closure of Golshor: Chapter 4.
  - Hohin's tablet about the widow in the rain: Chapter 4.
  - Shoterouro's public proclamation tablet read aloud at Mozram spring: Chapter 5.
  - The three tax-default tablets Nisiker presents to Hani: Chapter 9.
  - Shoterouro's personal journal tablets (multiple entries quoted in full): Chapters 8, 9.

## خشتهای رأی (Vote-bricks) — the council-vote system

- **Introduced at Khong-e-Azhdar (Ch.10)** and formalized at the shura scene (Ch.11): Shoterouro draws two symbols on two bricks — a flowing water line (representing acting on a proposal) and a horizontal line (representing standing still/not yet); a pile of small blank clay bricks are provided for people to place beside one symbol or the other, anonymously: "هر کس، فقط یک خشت بردارد... نامِ هیچکس خوانده نمیشود. فقط آخرِ کار، خشتهای هر دو سو را میشماریم." Evidence — Chapter 11.
- Individual named-topic bricks are also used to represent agenda items: "آب. جو. راه. انبار." Evidence — Chapter 11.
- Later, bricks are also inscribed with names of households (by Kuru) and carried by caravan to other villages to gather more input, alongside "گرهنامههای آب" (knotted water-agreements) — see Culture file for the knot-counting system.
- Shoterouro's own final brick bears "فقط نقش دستی... که گِل را فشرده بود" (only the imprint of a hand that had pressed the clay) — Evidence — Chapter 12.
- Kuru's memorial brick, inscribed post-mortem: "کورو؛ سنگتراشی که شمشیر را زمین گذاشت و خشت برداشت." Evidence — Chapter 13.

## َپهْرِه (Pahreh)

- Footnote definition: Evidence — Chapter 3 — Quote: "-۱ پره؛ دوک؛ ابزاری ساده از جنس چوب یا آهن برای ریسیدن پنبه و پشم." (a spindle, wood or iron, for spinning cotton/wool)

## دستاس (Dastas — hand-mill)

- Two flat stones used by women to grind grain by hand at Golshor; recurring sound-motif ("سنگ بر سنگ") through the caravanserai scenes. Evidence — Chapter 3, 6.
- Raju notes an alternative technology from Sind: "در سِند، جدیداً سنگها را آب میچرخاند." (water-driven millstones) Evidence — Chapter 3.

## Loom/weaving work (Raju's craft)

- Referenced generally ("نقش یا بافتهای از دستِ او") without further object-level detail. Evidence — Chapter 3.

## خشت (شکسته) — the broken brick found by Nika

- First picked up by Nika near a stonecutter at Kolfarah: "کنار پای سنگتراش تنومندی که پایینتر بود، خشت شکستهای افتاده بود. نیمهاش گم شده و لبههایش خُرد شده بود." She keeps it: "نیکا به سنگ نگاه کرد، مکثی کرد و خشت را در بقچه گذاشت." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- Becomes her grave marker, inscribed with a boat, water, and sun. Evidence — Chapter 7.
