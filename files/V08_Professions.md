# V08 — Professions / Occupations Depicted

Each entry lists the profession/occupation as depicted, the character(s)
shown performing it (if named), and the evidence.

## نغمهخوان (Traveling singer/musician)

- Nika. Called "نغمهخوان" by a worker at Kolfarah: "آه، نغمهخوان رسید. امروز چی میخونی؟" Evidence — Chapter 1.

## کوتور (Kotor — local ruler/guardian)

- Hani. Footnote: "به ایالمی: مراقب، محافظ و فرمانروا؛ فرمانروای محلی." Evidence — Chapter 1.

## وزیر (Vizier/minister)

- Shoterouro. Glossary: "شوترورو = وزیرِ هانی." Evidence — front matter.
- Also functions as royal scribe (دبیر) — see below; and later described explicitly as "همون دبیرِ شاهه" by bystanders. Evidence — Chapter 10.

## نانوا/baker (unnamed as formal title, but occupation shown)

- Mamuru, seen baking barley and acorn-flour bread at a tanoor. Evidence — Chapter 1.

## سنگتراش (Stonecutter/sculptor)

- Multiple unnamed stonecutters carve the Kolfarah relief. Evidence — Chapter 1.
- Kuru is explicitly a former stonecutter ("مردی بود با شانههایی پهن و دستانی که هنوز جای تیشه سنگتراشی بر آن مانده بود... سالها با همین دستها دیوار ساخته و گندم درو کرده بود"). Evidence — Chapter 5.
- His epitaph names the trade directly: "کورو؛ سنگتراشی که شمشیر را زمین گذاشت و خشت برداشت." Evidence — Chapter 13.
- Another unnamed young sculptor at Eshkaft-e-Salman comments that ordinary people are absent from the reliefs. Evidence — Chapter 12.

## کاهن (Priest/high priest)

- Present in the Narsina temple scene (کاهن اعظم = high priest, and a second/"دوم" priest) who debate Nika's presence. Evidence — Chapter 1.
- Halazh is referred to as having shouted a famous line "در معبد شوش" and is later described as a "کاهن" who is arrested: "اون کاهن رو بردن." Evidence — Chapter 3, 8. (Whether Halazh's formal role was always priestly, or this is how villagers came to describe him, is not further clarified — text simply calls him "کاهن" at the point of arrest.)
- Two unnamed priests observe the shura process at Eshkaft-e-Salman and remark on losing judicial authority. Evidence — Chapter 11.

## سرباز (Soldier)

- Numerous unnamed soldiers throughout; Kuru is a soldier who was formerly a stonecutter (see above).
- A "جوانترین سرباز" (youngest soldier) is a recurring minor figure who shows sympathy toward Nika (loosens her binding, escorts Manzat). Evidence — Chapters 1–2.

## دهقان (Farmer/peasant)

- Numerous unnamed farmers throughout, e.g. the farmer punished by Hani's father (Ch.1), farmers plowing near Kohbad (Ch.1, with oxen and wooden plow — گاوآهن چوبی).

## چوپان (Shepherd)

- Numerous unnamed shepherds throughout (e.g. the shepherd who helps fix Kourash's cart, Chapter 3; shepherds playing the ney, Chapters 5, 8, 12).
- گاپون (Gapon) is glossed as "چوپان گاو" (cattle-herder), distinct from a general shepherd. Evidence — Chapter 3 footnote.

## ارابهدار (Cart/caravan driver)

- Kourash. Evidence — Chapter 3 onward.

## مباشر (Steward/overseer)

- The unnamed estate steward at Kohbad who collects the levy (Ch.1).
- Hotran, described as "مباشرِ ارباب و کاخ" at Ayapir, tracking debtors on tablets. Evidence — Chapter 4, 11.

## خزانهدار اعظم (Grand Treasurer)

- Nisiker. Evidence — Chapter 9.

## دبیر/کاتب (Scribe)

- Shoterouro functions as the palace's principal scribe.
- Unnamed کاتبان (scribes) argue over grain accounts at Kolfarah (Ch.1) and record place-names/rider-counts at a Zagros way-station (Ch.5): "هر پیکی که میرسید، نام آبادی و شمار سواران را روی لوحی تازه مینوشت."
- A scribe at the start of the Shileyti raid writes "یاغیان مقاومت کردند" before fighting begins. Evidence — Chapter 7.

## بافنده (Weaver)

- Raju, whose "نقش یا بافتهای" (patterns/weavings) are found in many Zagros villages. Evidence — Chapter 3.

## آهنگر (Blacksmith)

- An unnamed itinerant/"آواره" blacksmith is seen at Golshor with a burned finger, saying "آهن اگر نسوزاند، نان هم نمیدهد." Evidence — Chapter 3.

## پیشگو (Fortune-teller)

- An unnamed old man with a sheepskin runs a fortune-telling stall at Kolfarah. Evidence — Chapter 1.

## دوکریس / پشمریس (Spinner)

- An unnamed woman at Golshor is repeatedly seen spinning wool with a پَهْرِه (spindle). Evidence — Chapter 3.

## گیاهدرمانگر/عطار-مانند (Herbalist-type knowledge, not formally titled)

- Ani (Anieynoma) is shown surrounded by dried herbs, a stone mortar, and organized medicinal bags, and later tends wounds; no formal title given in-text. Evidence — Chapter 5, 7, 10.

## نانپز (Baker, plural context)

- Multiple unnamed women baking bread are shown at Golshor and Eshkaft-e-Salman. Evidence — Chapters 3, 6, 12.

## آش/باقلهفروش (Stew/bean-soup vendor)

- Ebram-Baghleh, selling fava-bean stew near the councils. Evidence — Chapter 12.

## رقصنده (Dancer, informal)

- Napahun is described as a good dancer. Evidence — Chapter 12.

## UNKNOWN

- Whether any of these are hereditary/guild-based occupations, or how one entered a given profession, is UNKNOWN — the text gives no such detail beyond what is quoted above.
