# V10 — Visual Design Reference

This file exists to prevent visual-continuity errors in any derivative
illustration/adaptation work: it lists every explicitly stated physical
detail of characters, costumes, and key set-pieces, chapter-sourced. Where
no visual detail is given for an otherwise-important character (e.g. Nika's
face, hair, or exact age), that is marked UNKNOWN — illustrators must not
invent and present it as canon.

## Costume/material notes (general, not character-specific)

- Leather containers: خیگ (with wool/fleece, "پشم دار") vs. مَشک (without wool, "بیپشم") — distinguished explicitly by hide type. Evidence — Chapter 1 footnote.
- Soldiers' gear includes spears (نیزه), shields (سپر), swords (شمشیر), helmets (کالهخود), armor (زره).
- Villagers/rebels are repeatedly described as armed only with tools: بیل (spade), داس (sickle), تیشه (adze/chisel), گرزهای چوبی (wooden clubs), نیزههای چوپانی (shepherd's spears/poles). Evidence — Chapter 7 (Shileyti raid) — Quote: "مردها و زنها با بیل، داس، تیشه و گرزهای چوبی و نیزههای چوپانی به سوی گردنه دویدند."
- The Khong-e-Azhdar attackers ("چماقدارها") are specifically noted wearing "گردنبندهایِ استخوانی با نشان تیروتیر و ناهونته" (bone necklaces marked with the emblems of Tirutir and Nahunte). Evidence — Chapter 10.

## Key set-pieces — explicit visual data

### The Kolfarah relief (as being carved, in-story)
- Shows Hani "با تاج و ردایی فاخر و بلند" (with a crown and a fine, long robe), soldiers, captives, and sacrificial cattle below; seven naked captives ("هفت اسیرِ برهنه") staring outward from the stone; an inscription above. Evidence — Chapter 1.

### The Kolfarah relief (Appendix, art-historical description)
- Main relief: Hani (round Elamite cap) beside Shoterouro; lower register shows cattle/goat sacrifice. A second relief ("نقشبرجسته چهارسو") shows four men carrying a god's statue with unnamed captives at the border. Evidence — Appendix.

### The Eshkaft-e-Salman reliefs (Appendix)
- Four reliefs: (1) Hani with Hohin, children, and Shoterouro; (2) Hani with family, without the vizier; (3) Hani alone with an extensive inscription; (4) an important Elamite figure performing a rite. Evidence — Appendix.

### The Khong-e-Azhdar rock (Appendix)
- One worn Elamite-era relief (public gathering/audience scene) and one Parthian-era relief (Mehrdad II on horseback, receiving legitimacy via two dove-borne rings), carved on the same slab. Evidence — Appendix.

### Ata's two displayed hides (Golshor)
- First: shows an axe-maker, a hide-sewing woman, wood-gathering children. Evidence — Chapter 6.
- Second: shows laborers carrying loads under the sun, one figure holding a whip, another seated under a sunshade. Evidence — Chapter 6.
