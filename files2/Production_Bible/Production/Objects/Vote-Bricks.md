# Object Sheet: Vote-bricks

## Owner
Collective/communal — used by the shura (council) participants at Khong-e-Azhdar and Eshkaft-e-Salman; system devised/administered by Shoterouro. (Source: V05_Objects.md, V07_Culture.md)

## Material
Clay bricks (خشت), small and blank, provided in a pile for participants. (Source: V05_Objects.md)

## Appearances
- Chapter 10 — introduced at Khong-e-Azhdar. (V05_Objects.md, V03_Events.md E53)
- Chapter 11 — formalized: Shoterouro draws two symbols (flowing water line = act; horizontal line = wait) on two bricks; participants place one blank brick beside a symbol, anonymously. Named-topic bricks also used for agenda items ("آب. جو. راه. انبار."). (V05_Objects.md, V07_Culture.md)
- Chapter 12 — Kuru inscribes household names on bricks; Shoterouro's own brick bears only a handprint. (V05_Objects.md)
- Chapter 13 — Kuru's memorial brick, inscribed post-mortem: "کورو؛ سنگتراشی که شمشیر را زمین گذاشت و خشت برداشت." (V05_Objects.md)

## Events
E53, E59, E61, E68, E73, E76. (Source: V03_Events.md)

## Meaning (only as explicitly stated in Story Bible)
- Anonymity is an explicit stated rule: "نامِ هیچکس خوانده نمیشود. فقط آخرِ کار، خشتهای هر دو سو را میشماریم." (V05_Objects.md)
- Vote-bricks are carried by caravan, alongside knotted water-agreements, to extend the shura process to other villages. (V05_Objects.md, V07_Culture.md)
