## نیکا (Nika)

- **Role in story:** Traveling singer/musician (نغمهخوان) who plays a چنگ (chang, a harp-like stringed instrument); becomes a symbol of popular voice.
- **Age:** UNKNOWN
- **Height/Weight:** UNKNOWN
- **Etymology given in-text (footnote):** Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "۱ـ در زبان ایالمی: Nika: (ما)" (footnote glosses the name "Nika" in "Elamite" as meaning "ما"/"we")
- **Home:** کُهباد (Kohbad) — lives in a house there; neighbor is تِال. Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "در یکی از این خانهها، نیکا از خواب برخاست."
- **Instrument:** Owns a چنگ (chang). Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "چنگ را از روی طاقچه برداشت و کنار پنجره ایستاد."
- **Family/household referenced:** Lives among households counted by Tela as under threat of increased tax: "سه... من، دُلو کودَر و ننه رَکو." (Nika's own household is one of the three, per the dialogue context — Chapter 1.)
- **Writes on walls with charcoal:** Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "سپس، از خانه بیرون آمد و تکهای زغال برداشت و روی دیوار خانه نوشت: «این سه خانه را کسی فراموش نکند»."
- **First confrontation with Hani (in the نارسینا temple at کولْفرح):** Sings in the temple; when Hani asks "به این نیایش میگویی؟!" she replies "نه. این نیایش نیست... صدای مردم..." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Arrested/imprisoned:** Taken by Hani's soldiers after the temple exchange. Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "ـ او را ببرید. سربازها جلو آمدند."
- **Held bound in a cave near کولْفرح:** wrists injured by rope. Evidence — Chapter 2 — Quote: "مچهایش از فشار طناب زخم شده بود و خون خشکشده روی ساعدهایش به رنگ قهوهای تیره درآمده بود."
- **Freed** by a group led by موراد (Moraad), during which the old man هیش (Hish) is killed by a soldier's spear. Evidence — Chapter 2.
- **Confession before death (to آنی):** Reveals she once betrayed refugees' hiding place a year earlier in دِهْمورْد و اِشکفتجاموشی, resulting in three deaths, and that only she knew until now. Evidence — Chapter 7 — Quote: "یک سال پیش... در دِهْمورْد و اِشکفتجاموشی... جای پناهندهها را لو دادم... فکر میکردم جان بقیه را نجات میدهم... سه نفر کشته شدند... فقط من میدانستم... حاال تو هم میدانی."
- **Death:** Killed in the raid on Shileyti — struck first by a tomahawk/axe-handle to the shoulder, then two arrows (one in the wounded shoulder, one in the chest). Evidence — Chapter 7 — Quote: "اسبی از پشت رسید... دستهی تبر بود که به شانهاش خورد... تیری از دلِ تاریکی رسید و در همان شانهی زخمخورده نشست... تیر دیگری از تاریکی آمد و به سینه نیکا نشست." No one is stated to know whose bow fired: "هیچکس نفهمید تیرها از کمانِ چه کسی رها شد."
- **Last words / requests before death:** asks آنی whether a person can be remembered "with all their faults" ("هنوز میشه آدم رو با همه خطاهاش به یاد آورد؟"); asks that the chang be given to Manzat with instructions "هر وقت قایقش راهش را گم کرد، چنگ بزند. صدا... راه را پیدا میکند."; asks "زمین نامم را پس نمیزند؟" Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Burial:** Buried beside the چشمه (spring) at Shileyti under an old oak; no headstone, only the broken خشت (mudbrick) she had once drawn a boat/water/sun on. Evidence — Chapter 7 — Quote: "فقط خشت شکستهای را روی خاک گذاشتند که قبالً نیکا روی آن چیزی کشیده بود: قایق، آب، خورشید."
- **Posthumous statement (quoted by townsfolk):** "اگر بمیرم، چنگم را نگه دارید. صدا میماند." Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Grandmother (unnamed) referenced in memory:** told Nika that stone does not forget what is entrusted to it. Evidence — Chapter 2 — Quote: "نیکا لبخند زد. یادش آمد مادربزرگش همیشه میگفت سنگ، آنچه را به او سپرده شود، فراموش نمیکند."

## هانی پسر تاهیهی (Hani, son of Tahihi)

- **Title/role:** Local ruler ("کوتورِ آیاپیر") of Ayapir; historically-grounded figure per author's note. Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "حاال که خودش «کوتورِ آیاپیر» شده بود."
- **Footnote gloss of "کوتور":** Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "-۱ به ایالمی: مراقب، محافظ و فرمانروا؛ فرمانروای محلی."
- **Residence:** کاخ سنگی زاپوالسیمی (the stone palace of Zapol-e-Simi). Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Father:** تاهیهی (unnamed further); shown in a childhood memory punishing a farmer for late tax, saying "قحطی به خدایان ربط دارد نه به ما." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Wife:** هوهین (Hohin). Evidence — Chapter 3 — Quote: "او هوهین، همسر هانی بود."
- **Children:** Referenced only via appendix rock-relief description (see V10/Appendix): "هانی، پیکر خود و هوهین، زن و خواهر محبوبم، و فرزندم را برای مادر تاریشا نصب کردم" — number/names of children UNKNOWN.
- **Childhood memory:** Recalls, as a boy, hearing a farmer's wife crying after his father imprisoned her husband for late tax payment; wanted to bring the man bread but did not leave his room; the farmer was gone by morning. Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Orders the search for Nika after her escape:** "شیلهیتی را بگردید... اما بیهوده خون نریزید." Evidence — Chapter 4.
- **Reads Hohin's tablet (about a widow searching for fodder in the rain)** and hides it under his "فرمانها" (orders); rereads the closing line "آیا چیزی جز گرفتن، از ما دیدهاند؟" the next morning. Evidence — Chapter 4.
- **After Nika's death, travels to Shileyti and declares an آتشبس (ceasefire):** kneels, takes a handful of earth, says "برای نگه داشتن این خاک، خیلی آدم کشته شد." then declares: "آتشبس... امروز نه سخن از پیروزی است، نه از شکست... بگذارید نخست گندم برگردد." Evidence — Chapter 8.
- **Kuru (a soldier) throws down his sword before Hani** in protest at Nika's death; Hani picks it up and returns it to Kuru. Evidence — Chapter 8.
- **Later refuses the treasurer نیسیکِر's advice to burn villages and reassert control by force**, saying "نه... به جای اولش برنمیگردد" and ultimately "دیگر هیچ دهی آتش زده نمیشود." Evidence — Chapter 9.
- **The army commander effectively defects** ("فرمانده رفته پیشِ شاهزادههای شوش و جوبَجِی... فرمانده دیگه از هانی فرمان نمیبره") and نیسیکِر relinquishes the treasury seal, saying "خزانه دیگر از فرمانِ من بیرون است... و از فرمانِ تو هم... هر که سپاه را داشته باشد... خزانه هم مالِ اوست." Evidence — Chapter 9.
- **Departs the palace** with Kourash's caravan, taking up a hoe/bill (بیل) and sitting beside a goat, saying he is going "هر جا کاری باشد" (wherever there is work). Evidence — Chapter 9.
- **After his departure**, described only through rumor: "یکی میگفت او را کنار جویی دیده، دیگری میگفت با کاروانی از گردنه گذشته است. هیچکس نمیدانست کدام روایت درست است." Evidence — Chapter 9. His subsequent fate/whereabouts: UNKNOWN (not narrated further in Chapters 10–13).

## شوترورو (Shoterouro)

- **Role:** Glossary states: "وزیرِ هانی" (Hani's vizier/minister). Evidence — front-matter glossary.
- Acts as scribe/record-keeper (writes on clay tablets — لوح) for Hani throughout.
- **Appearance in Chapter 1:** Enters the نارسینا temple with Hani and soldiers; reads a tablet aloud recounting Hani's victory over "۲۰ تن از سرکردگان شورشیان." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Sent with کورو to hunt for Nika through the Zagros** after her escape (Chapter 5); wears disguise in markets ("لباس مُبدل") in Chapter 4.
- **Returns to Ayapir alone**, leaving کورو to continue the search, saying: "هانی باید بداند در زاگرس چه میگذرد." Evidence — Chapter 5.
- **Writes a first-person journal entry** the night of the ceasefire, reflecting on decades of writing conquest tallies, breaking a clay tablet, and resolving that he may stop recording death counts. Signed "شوترورو." Evidence — Chapter 8 (full text, "از نوشتههای شوترورو").
- **Later becomes a resident of Golshor** ("سه ساله که گُلِشوری شده... حاال دیگه قلمش حرف مردم رو مینویسه") — three years after the ceasefire, per two men's remark at Khong-e-Azhdar. Evidence — Chapter 10.
- **Physically attacked at the Khong-e-Azhdar assembly:** struck with a thrown stone on the shoulder, then a stick on the arm, by grieving villagers; does not fight back or fall; says "سنگ، آدم را همیشه به زمین نمیاندازد؛ فقط جایِ آن میماند... بعضی زخمها باید بمانند؛ تا آدم فراموش نکند." Evidence — Chapter 10.
- **Writes the epitaph-line for Kuru's death** at the end (see Kuru entry) and a final aphorism: "بر سنگ، نامِ شاهان ماند؛ اما راه را دستهایی ساختند که نامی نداشتند." Evidence — Chapter 9 (final line of chapter).
- **Digs irrigation ditches alongside villagers** by Chapter 11 ("تا زانو در گلِ جوی فرو رفته بود").

## مامورو (Mamuru)

- **Role:** Baker at Kohbad, bakes bread by the tanoor at the story's opening. Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "مامورو پای تنور خم شده بود و نان میپخت."
- **Etymology footnote:** Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "-۱ در زبان ایالمی: Amma-muru (مادر - خاک)؛ مادرِ خاک." (glossed as "Amma-muru," "mother - earth"; "Mother of the Earth")
- Gives bread to a barefoot child chasing a goat: "بگیر، بخور." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Present at Khong-e-Azhdar assembly** distributing dried bread among children; states "اول شکم باید سیر بشه. آدمِ گرسنه حرف سرش نمیشه." Evidence — Chapter 10.
- **Central conciliating voice during the stoning incident:** stops the crowd from beating the captured club-men, saying "اگر امروز ما هم همین کار را بکنیم... فردا چه فرقی با آنها خواهیم داشت؟" Evidence — Chapter 10.
- **Present in the شورا (council) scenes at Eshkaft-e-Salman**, helps organize the vote-brick process; described marking approval by nodding, rarely speaking. Evidence — Chapter 11.

## تِال (Tela)

- **Role:** Nika's neighbor at Kohbad, described wearing patched clothing. Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "همسایهشان، تِال، با جامهای پر از وصله، از کوچه صدایش زد."
- Reports increased tax/levy (خراج) demand from the estate steward. Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Speaks at Eshkaft-e-Salman assembly** using a metaphor of a tree and its roots to argue against the "upstream" farmer's claim to more water: "ریشه اگه آب رو برای خودش نگه داره، درخت نمیمونه؛ خودش هم نمیمونه." Evidence — Chapter 12.

## مانْزات (Manzat) — the boy

(Distinct from the goddess Manzat listed in the appendix pantheon table — see V09_Religion.)

- **Role:** A boy first met by Nika at Kolfarah, playing with a small boat made from an oak leaf. Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "همانجا پسربچهای با برگ بلوط، قایق کوچکی میساخت."
- **Origin stated by the boy himself:** "از آن طرف کوه... از جایی که فقط دودش مانده." (from the other side of the mountain, a place of which only smoke remains) Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Arrived with a group ("گروه") the night before** meeting Nika: "ما دیشب رسیدیم." Evidence — Chapter 1.
- **Present in the temple when Nika is arrested**; follows the rescue party afterward and gives Nika back her chang. Evidence — Chapter 1–2.
- **Present at Nika's death** in Shileyti; receives the chang per Nika's dying instruction relayed through Ani. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Later custodian of the chang alongside Ani**; carries the chang in a bundle on a mule; plays it once secretly at night after Nika's death. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Continues to make and float oak-leaf boats** throughout the later chapters (Ch. 8–13), a recurring motif tied to him specifically.
- **Present through Chapters 10–13** at the councils; hands the chang to Ani to be played publicly at Khong-e-Azhdar (Chapter 10) and again at Eshkaft-e-Salman, where a girl (unnamed) ultimately takes over playing it. Evidence — Chapter 12.
- **At the end of Chapter 13**, present when Kuru is shot and dies; is given a final small brick by Kuru ("این یکی مالِ تو") and told to write on it "هرچه دیدی... نه هرچه بهت گفتند."

## موراد (Moraad)

- **Role:** Leader of the group that frees Nika from the cave. Evidence — Chapter 2 — Quote: "نیمهشب، موراد با نیزهای چوبی که هنوز بوی گله میداد، از میان بلوطها بیرون آمد."
- Organizes the rescue tactically: "دو نفر دهانه را بگیرند. یکی سمت باد، یکی آن طرف سایه. بقیه، پشت سرِ من." Evidence — Chapter 2.
- **Present at Nika's death**; described as having worked alongside the slain Hish for years digging ditches, cutting oak, driving herds through passes. Evidence — Chapter 2 (memory), Chapter 7 (death scene): "مشتش را چنان بر خاک کوبید که بند انگشتانش شکافت."
- **Visits Nika's grave nightly** after her death, per Manzat's observation. Evidence — Chapter 8.
- **Physically restrains and dunks captured club-men in the river** at Khong-e-Azhdar; later releases one at Mamuru's urging. Evidence — Chapter 10.
- Recurs through Chapters 9–13 as an organizer of irrigation-ditch work and a voice at the councils; states near the end: "تاج که بیفتد، گاهی فرمانروایی با نامی دیگر برمیگردد." Evidence — Chapter 13.

## هیش (Hish)

- **Role:** An old man, part of the rescue party for Nika; killed by a soldier's thrown spear at the cave entrance during the rescue. Evidence — Chapter 2 — Quote: "همان لحظه، نیزه به سینه پیرمرد خورد. نیزه که نشست، زانوهایش خم شد و روی خاک افتاد و خون از زیر دستش جاری شد."
- **Named** only after death, when Nika asks his name and Moraad answers "هیش." Evidence — Chapter 2.
- **Object connected to him:** a sickle (داس) with a worn handle, later seen held by a woman in black at the Khong-e-Azhdar assembly. Evidence — Chapter 10 — Quote: "داسی با دسته رنگ و رو رفته را در دست داشت؛ داسی که به پیرمرد نیزهخورده در شبِ رهایی نیکا تعلق داشت." (The woman's identity/relation to Hish: UNKNOWN — not stated.)
- **News of the attack in Ayapir bazaar** later also carries his name alongside Nika's. Evidence — Chapter 4.

## کورو (Kuru)

- **Role:** A soldier, formerly a stonemason; footnote glosses the name. Evidence — Chapter 5 — Quote: "۱ـ در ایالمی: Kuru: شاخ (مبارزه)" (glossed "Kuru" as "horn / combat" in "Elamite").
- **Physical description:** "مردی بود با شانههایی پهن و دستانی که هنوز جای تیشه سنگتراشی بر آن مانده بود." Evidence — Chapter 5.
- **Background stated:** "سالها با همین دستها دیوار ساخته و گندم درو کرده بود؛ حاال شمشیر به کمر داشت." Evidence — Chapter 5.
- **Sent with شوترورو to hunt Nika** through the Zagros (Chapter 5); continues alone after Shoterouro returns to Ayapir.
- **Present at Nika's death** in Shileyti; shouts "ایست!" as the arrows are fired but is too late; turns and rides for Ayapir to report. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Throws down his sword before Hani** at the ceasefire, declaring "من برای جنگ آمده بودم، نه برای کشتنِ کسی مثل نیکا." Hani returns the sword to him. Evidence — Chapter 8.
- **States to Hohin he cannot serve as "the sword of the one who killed Nika," even if he forgives Hani.** Evidence — Chapter 8.
- **Present through Chapters 10–13**; tends to the wounded during the stoning incident (Chapter 10); is struck on the forehead by a thrown stone but does not fall.
- **Writes names of households on the vote-bricks** in the caravan-preparation scene, Chapter 13.
- **Death:** Shot with an arrow in the chest while walking beside the departing caravan of bricks, from an unseen assailant near the mountain pass. Evidence — Chapter 13 — Quote: "پرِ تیر از چلوار کهنهاش بیرون زده بود... زانوهایش خم شد." Last words: "قایقت را... گم نکن، پسرم... آب... راهش را... پیدا میکند." Buried epitaph, written by Shoterouro at the crowd's dictation: "کورو؛ سنگتراشی که شمشیر را زمین گذاشت و خشت برداشت." Evidence — Chapter 13.

## کوراش (Kourash)

- **Role:** A caravan/cart driver (ارابهدار), first introduced steering a cart stuck in mud on the way to Golshor. Evidence — Chapter 3.
- Owns mules; addresses one directly ("تو ساکت باش. سهم تو کمتر از بقیه نیست."). Evidence — Chapter 3.
- **Recurs across nearly every chapter from Ch.3 onward** as a transporter of goods, people, and news; brings news of Nika's escape to the Ayapir bazaar (Chapter 4: "نیکا گریخت!").
- **Present at Khong-e-Azhdar and Eshkaft-e-Salman councils**; picks up a mutilated/ear-cropped dog ("سگِ گوشبریدهای") and sets it on the council table as a joke, saying "این رو هم عضو کنین. تا دزدِ شورا رو بگیره!" Evidence — Chapter 11.
- **Organizes the caravan of vote-bricks and seed at the end of Ch.12–13**, mapping out the routes to be visited: "اول دزپارت... این شاخه میرود مُنج، این یکی لُردِگون و سرخون..." Evidence — Chapter 12.
- Present when Kuru is shot dead at the end of Chapter 13; described adjusting the mule's bell in silence afterward.

## راجو (Raju)

- **Role:** A traveler from a land called سِند (Sind), a weaver; repeatedly compares Zagros customs to those of Sind. Evidence — Chapter 3.
- **Description of trade:** "حاال کمتر دهی در زاگرس بود که نقش یا بافتهای از دستِ او در آن دیده نشود." (weaving/carpet work) Evidence — Chapter 3.
- **Recounts a custom from Sind** of villagers gathering yearly, without a king, to decide how to divide water: "برای اینکه تصمیم بگیرند آب را چطور تقسیم کنند... بدون شاه." Evidence — Chapter 3.
- Present at the Golshor gatherings in Chapter 6 discussing the buffalo-hide picture Ata displays.

## آتا (Ata)

- **Role:** An old man at the Golshor caravanserai, described as listening more than speaking, but asking the caravanserai's "most dangerous questions." Evidence — Chapter 3 — Quote: "مثل همیشه، بیشتر گوش میداد تا حرف بزند... قدیمیها میدانستند، خطرناکترین پرسشهای گلشور معموالً از دهان او بیرون میآید."
- **Father of سِلو.** Evidence — Chapter 3 — Quote: "پسرش سِلو کنار او بود."
- **Displays a hide (پوست) with a picture** depicting ordinary laborers (axe-maker, hide-sewer, wood-gathering children) instead of kings, asking "چرا نامِ آنها روی سنگ نیست؟" Evidence — Chapter 6.
- Present at Eshkaft-e-Salman scenes in Ch.12.

## سِلو (Selo)

- **Role:** Ata's son, at Golshor; described sharpening a spade handle with a knife, avoiding eye contact while thinking, scraping wood before speaking. Evidence — Chapter 3.
- **Backstory line:** "همان دستهایی که روزگاری قلم و لوح گرفته بودند و حاال دسته بیل" (his hands once held pen and tablet, now hold a spade) — implying a former scribe/administrative role, though this is not elaborated further. Evidence — Chapter 3.
- Speaks against inherited hierarchy at Golshor; later takes an active organizing role in the water-shura at Khong-e-Azhdar and Eshkaft-e-Salman (Ch.10–12), e.g. "خوب. اولین مسئله آب."

## جَبْرو (Jabro)

- **Role:** A physically strong man at Golshor, carries heavy sacks easily; described lifting anything ("از کیسه گندم گرفته تا کودک همسایه"). Evidence — Chapter 3.
- Comic figure; consistent through Chapters 6–13 as a helper/laborer and occasional comic relief ("انگار این کیسه خیال کرده... دوشم خونهشه").
- **At Khong-e-Azhdar, physically carries and dunks a captured club-man into the river ("شط").** Evidence — Chapter 10.
- Joins the final caravan to visit other villages at the end of Ch.12.

## هَالژ (Halazh)

- **Role:** An itinerant figure carrying scrolls/messages between villages (Golshor, لَهْسون, etc.); reputed to have shouted in the Shush temple: "خدا را بیشتر در دستهای مردم دیدهام تا در این سنگها." Evidence — Chapter 3.
- Brings a coded scroll from لَهْسون describing a village council practice around water and dates. Evidence — Chapter 3.
- **Arrested during the ceasefire period**, per a traveler's report: "اون کاهن رو بردن... همونی که خرقهشو انداخت رو خاک... هَالژ؟... آره. دستبسته بردنش." His fate afterward is reported only as rumor that Hani did not order his killing but sent him "به دادگاهِ دربار" (to the court's tribunal). Evidence — Chapter 8. Final fate: UNKNOWN (not narrated further).
- **His name appears written on a vote-brick** at the end (Chapter 13): "روی آن تنها نامِ «هَالژ» مانده بود."

## هوهین (Hohin)

- **Role:** Wife of Hani. Evidence — Chapter 3 — Quote: "او هوهین، همسر هانی بود."
- **Habit at Golshor:** visits periodically, listens more than she speaks, and studies people's hands before responding: "پیش از هر پاسخ، چند لحظه به دستهای گوینده نگاه میکرد؛ انگار حرف را از کف دستها میخواند، نه از دهان." Evidence — Chapter 3.
- **Keeps her own tablets/records** of things she witnesses among the people (e.g., the widow in the rain, Chapter 4).
- **After the ceasefire, described as "کسی که دیگر کمتر او را همسرِ هانی میخواند"** (someone fewer people now called "Hani's wife") — attends councils and does manual labor (carrying water skins, milking, being kicked by a cow) at Khong-e-Azhdar. Evidence — Chapter 10.
- **Present through Ch.10–13**, active at the councils, treats the injured after the stoning (Chapter 10), and remains at the settlement's council while others travel onward at the end of Ch.12: "همه که نمیتوانیم برویم. شورا باید همینجا هم بماند."
- Appendix (rock relief description, Eshkaft-e-Salman): described alongside Hani in an inscription: "من، هانی، پیکر خود و هوهین، زن و خواهر محبوبم، و فرزندم را برای مادر تاریشا نصب کردم." Note: the inscription calls her "زن و خواهر محبوبم" (my beloved wife and sister) — stated as historical/epigraphic content in the Appendix, distinct from the novel's narrative voice.

## هوتْران (Hotran)

- **Role:** Steward/overseer (مباشرِ ارباب و کاخ) — collects debts/tax records in the Ayapir bazaar; introduced walking with a tax tablet, tracking a list of debtors. Evidence — Chapter 4.
- **Recurs at the shura formation in Chapter 11**, objecting to collective governance: "اربابِ بد بهتر از شورایی است که هرکس به سویی بکشد." When asked what he can contribute, answers "حساب و کتاب" (accounting).
- **Declines to cast a vote-brick** ("رأی نمیدهم. من به شما اعتماد ندارم. اما میمانم و حسابها را نگاه میکنم.") but continues attending. Evidence — Chapter 11.
- **Still present, still uncommitted, at the caravan's departure** in Chapter 13: "نمیدانم. شاید... شاید هنوز زود باشد که باور کنم." Watches the caravan leave until it's out of sight.

## نیسیکِر (Nisiker)

## آنیْنوما / آنی (Anieynoma / Ani)

- **Role:** A woman at Shileyti; introduced awake at night reading an old tablet, surrounded by dried thyme, oak resin, a stone mortar, and neatly arranged bags of mountain herbs — implying herbal/healing knowledge, though no title is given. Evidence — Chapter 5.
- **Tends Nika's fatal wound** during the raid: tears cloth from her own hem to press on the wound. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Present at Nika's death**; is told Nika's final secret (the betrayal a year prior) and keeps it. Evidence — Chapter 7.
- **Becomes keeper of Nika's chang** alongside Manzat; plays it publicly for the first time, unprompted, once villagers resume rebuilding (Chapter 9): "این بار، بیآن که کسی از او بخواهد، نواخت."
- **Present through Chapters 10–13** at the councils; tends an injured old man during the stoning incident (Chapter 10).
- **At Eshkaft-e-Salman, hands the chang onward** — an unnamed teenage girl eventually takes over playing it publicly, after which "دیگر هیچکس نگفت چنگِ نیکاست... میگفتند: چنگِ میدان است." (see Objects file for the chang's full object-history.) Evidence — Chapter 12.

## چیشپیش (Chishpish)

- Points to a "شاهین" (falcon) image and tells children it is "خدای پارسها" (god of the Persians) and "نگهبان پیمانها" (guardian of covenants). Evidence — Chapter 4.

## ناپهون (Napahun)

- **Role:** A man described as a good dancer, "همیشه تشنه" (always thirsty), with scars on his hands, present at Eshkaft-e-Salman. Evidence — Chapter 12.
- Footnote gloss of the name: Evidence — Chapter 12 — Quote: "۱ـ به ایالمی: خدا: nap یا شکل بزرگتر آن napir + نور/روشنایی: hun" (name glossed as combining "god" (nap/napir) and "light/brightness" (hun) in "Elamite").
- Dances/stamps rhythmically without speaking at first ("وقتی زبان را میبندند، بدن حرف میزند."); Kourash joins him; Hohin later hands him a cup (پیاله). Evidence — Chapter 12.
