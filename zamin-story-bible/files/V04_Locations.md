# V04 — Locations

## کُهباد (Kohbad)

- **Type:** A village with an estate ("عمارت اربابی") with a grain store, farmland, wood-and-clay houses.
- Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "در آن روزِ مهآلود پاییزی، کُهباد پیش از برآمدنِ آفتاب نفس میکشید."
- **Contains:** the estate house/barn ("عمارت اربابی... انبار غله"), village houses of stone and clay with charcoal writing on the walls ("روی دیوارِ چند خانه از سنگ و گِل در حاشیه روستا، نوشتههایی با زغال دیده میشد"), Nika's and Tela's houses.
- Home village of Nika, Tela, and Mamuru.
- Referenced again later as home of "پیرمردی دانا از کُهباد" who explains the name "هَلْتَمتی" to children at Eshkaft-e-Salman. Evidence — Chapter 12.

## کولْفرح (Kolfarah)

- **Type:** Site of a temple to نارسینا (Narsina), with stone reliefs being carved of Hani's military victory; a cave used as a holding cell is located below/near the temple.
- Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "معبد نارسینا در سینه سنگ، با دهانه رو به دره پدیدار شد."
- Glossary entry: Evidence — front matter glossary — Quote: "کولفرح = نیایشگاه نارسینا"
- **Contains:** a market/work area ("محوطهی کولفرح") with stonecutters, scribes arguing over grain accounts, sheep, a rock face being carved with Hani's likeness and an inscription.
- Inscription quoted: Evidence — Chapter 1 — Quote: "«من، هانی پسر تاهیهی، این پیکره را در این مکان برپا کردم، زیرا (خدای) تیروتیر مرا شایسته دانست»..."
- **Appendix data (پیوست):** Described as "نیایشگاه نارسینا" with a main relief showing Hani (wearing a round Elamite cap/کاله گرد ایالمی) beside Shoterouro, a lower scene of cattle/goat sacrifice, and text: "«من، هانی، ۲۰ تن از سرکردگان شورشیان شیلهیتی را به اسارت درآوردم و خونشان را به کفاره ریختم. ۱۲۰ بز کوهی گرفتم و خونشان را در معبد قربانی کردم»." A second relief ("نقشبرجسته چهارسو") shows four men carrying a god's statue with unnamed captives in the border. Evidence — Appendix.
- Appendix states this is one of the author's stated real/historical sites. Evidence — Appendix, "یادداشت نویسنده."
- **Author's note explicitly:** the anonymous captives depicted here were a direct inspiration for the novel's central theme. Evidence — Appendix — Quote: "تصویر اسیران گمنام در حاشیه این نقشبرجسته و متن سنگنگاره این معبد، یکی از الهامهای اصلی شکلگیری مضمون مرکزی رمان بود: کسانی که در تاریخ حضور داشتهاند، اما نامشان باقی نمانده است."

## زاپوالسیمی (Zapol-e-Simi)

- **Type:** Hani's stone palace ("کاخ سنگی").
- Glossary entry: Evidence — front matter glossary — Quote: "زاپوالسیمی = کاخِ سنگیِ هانی در آیاپیر (نمادِ قلمرو و مرکزِ حکمرانیِ او)" — i.e. described in the glossary as symbol of his realm and seat of rule; located at/near Ayapir.
- **Contains:** cold stone corridors, Hani's bedchamber with a taghcheh (wall-niche) and lantern, a stable (استبل) with a war-horse tended by an old groom (مهترِ پیر), an ivan/veranda overlooking the mountains, a great hall (تاالرِ بزرگ) with a throne platform ("جایگاهِ شاه... همان سنگ، همان پلهها"), a rooftop.
- Evidence — Chapter 1, 4, 9 (various scenes set here).
- By Chapter 9's end, described as emptied of its former retinue: "جایی که روزی صفِ فرماندهان، کاهنان و دبیران تا پلهها میرسید، اکنون خالی بود. فقط چند لوحِ شکسته روی زمین افتاده بود."

## آیاپیر (Ayapir)

- **Type:** A settlement in a wide plain among the mountains, with a market/bazaar ("بازارِ کَشْدَره").
- Evidence — Chapter 4 — Quote: "آیاپیر در دشتی پهن میان کوهها نشسته بود؛ گویی زاگرس در آنجا شکافته شده بود تا جایی برای آب و آدم بماند."
- **Surrounding peaks named:** مُنْگَشت (to the southeast, casting shadow over the plain), الهَک و چیوه (watching over passes/old valleys). Evidence — Chapter 4.
- **Vegetation on slopes:** بلوط، کُنار و بنه (oak, sidr/jujube, wild pistachio); slopes turn green in spring. Evidence — Chapter 4.
- **Water:** "نهری فصلی با پیچوخم از میان آیاپیر میگذشت" (a seasonal, winding stream runs through Ayapir); water is stated to be scarce enough to cause disputes. Evidence — Chapter 4.
- **Market area named:** بازارِ کَشْدَره, with a wooden bridge over the stream; areas within/near the bazaar are named: کِفْتْ (wrestling/strongman area), کلدوزخ (game area with سُکُتْها), هَوْرِه (small marsh, with buffalo), دِیْمِه و لَهْجُزِی (theft scene location), سَراک (a mountain pass near Ayapir, from which cavalry departs).
- **Footnote glosses:** نونتِلی = unpaid day laborer ("کارگر بدون مزد و مواجب! کار فقط به ازای سیر شدن و زنده ماندن!"), هَوْرِه = "مانداب؛ مردابِ کوچک" (small marsh), سُکُتْ = a plant used to make brooms. Evidence — Chapter 4 footnotes.
- Appendix confirms Ayapir is the real site identified as modern ایذه (Izeh). Evidence — front-matter historical note — Quote: "هانی، فرمانروای آیاپیر (ایذهی کنونی)."
- Glossary: "آیاپیر = ایذهی امروزی." Evidence — front matter glossary.

## گُلِشور (Golshor)

- **Type:** A settlement in Zagros, reached via a mountain pass, centered on a caravanserai (کاروانسرا).
- First seen from above: Evidence — Chapter 3 — Quote: "غروب بود. ارابه از گردنه گذشت... چند سقف گِلی. طویلهها. دیوارهای سنگی."
- **Contains:** the caravanserai's great hall (تاالرِ بزرگ) with an oven/hearth, shelves of tablets and scrolls (طاقچههای پر از لوح و طومار), hand-mills (دستاس) operated by women squatting by the stream, a stable/trough area.
- Overlooking peak named: بَلیطبلند (mentioned as towering over the caravanserai during a storm). Evidence — Chapter 6.
- Site of extended recurring "town-square" debate scenes: Chapters 3 and 6.
- Shoterouro later settles here permanently ("سه ساله که گُلِشوری شده"). Evidence — Chapter 10.

## شیلهیتی (Shileyti)

- **Type:** A mountain region/village in Zagros; site of the war's key battle and Nika's death.
- Glossary entry: Evidence — front matter glossary — Quote: "شیلهیتی = منطقهای در زاگرس" (a region in Zagros).
- Also identified in the appended pre-story historical note as the Elamite name for the Karun river region, distinct usage: Note — the glossary lists "پیرین = نام ایالمیِ رود کارون" (Pirin = Elamite name of the Karun river) separately from Shileyti; a Chapter 1 footnote also glosses "پیرین" as "نام رودخانه کارون به زبان ایالمی."
- **Contains:** kapars/tents (کپرها), a spring (چشمه) beside a great oak (بلوط کهن) where Nika is buried, cliffs, sheep pens.
- Site of the tablet-list of the dead (E07), the raid and battle (E27), and Nika's grave, ceasefire declaration (E35), and continued rebuilding through Chapters 9–10.

## خونْگْاَژدر (Khong-e-Azhdar)

- **Type:** A site with ancient rock reliefs, described as located between other named landmarks in the Izeh plain.
- Evidence — Chapter 10 — Quote: "صخرههای خونْگْاَژدر از دلِ آن سر برآورده بودند؛ تیره و خاموش، مثل پیرمردانی که هزار سال است آمد و رفتِ آدمها را تماشا میکنند."
- Site of the first popular water-council assembly (Chapters 10–11), the stoning incident, and the vote-brick ritual (خشتهای رأی).
- **Appendix data:** "خونْگْاَژدر، کولفرح و اشکفتسلمان بخشی از دشت ایذه را در میان خود گرفته است؛ خونْگْاَژدر در نزدیکی ایذه، شامل دو نقشبرجسته از دورههای ایالمی و پارتی بر روی یک تختهسنگ است." One Elamite relief (now largely worn away) reportedly depicted "صحنهی بارعام یا گردهماییِ مردم در برابر شاه" — described in the appendix as one of the oldest known depictions of "جمع شدنِ مردم" for a political or ritual purpose. A second, Parthian-era relief shows Mehrdad II ("مهرداد دوم، شاه اشکانی") on horseback with two doves bearing a ring of power, granting legitimacy to a local ruler. The site is stated to have been used as an open-air shelter "از ایالمِ میانه تا دوران اشکانی." Evidence — Appendix.

## اِشکفتسلمان (Eshkaft-e-Salman)

- **Type:** A shrine/rock-relief site dedicated to تاریشا (Tarisha), reached about "دو سه ساعتی" of travel from the surrounding villages; becomes the final and larger seat of the popular council.
- Glossary entry: Evidence — front matter glossary — Quote: "اِشکفتسلمان = نیایشگاه تاریشا"
- Evidence — Chapter 12 — Quote: "سپیده هنوز کامل بر دامنههای مُنْگَشت ننشسته بود که مردم، از همان نخستین روشنای سحر، به راه افتاده بودند... راه، دو سه ساعتی بیشتر نبود."
- **Contains:** a spring, orchards ("آب و باغ هم دارد" per Mamuru), qanats needing clearance (الیروبی), rock reliefs, a درگاهِ معبدِ تاریشا (gateway of the Tarisha temple) with a small lamp lit by an old woman.
- **Appendix data:** Four described reliefs: (1) Hani beside Hohin, his children, and Shoterouro; (2) Hani with family, without the vizier; (3) Hani alone, with an extensive inscription; (4) an important Elamite figure performing a rite. Inscription text (relief 3, partial): "«من، هانی، پیکر خود و هوهین، زن و خواهر محبوبم، و فرزندم را برای مادر تاریشا نصب کردم»..." The appendix notes Hohin's presence beside Hani in this relief is stated to be "حضوری کمنظیر در هنر و سنگنگاری جهان باستان" (a rare occurrence in ancient world art/relief-carving). Evidence — Appendix.
- Site of the final chapters' council, the chang's handover to the unnamed girl, and the caravan's departure (and Kuru's death nearby).

## Other named places (mentioned but not scened in detail)

The following places are named in dialogue, footnotes, reports of ruin, or as origin-villages of assembly attendees, without further descriptive detail given. Evidence for each is the chapter of first mention:

- شوتور-نهونته (دربارِ شوتور-نهونته) — a court/place from which the lone rider comes at the story's opening. Chapter 1.
- تَکاب، بَردْبُران — villages whose people join Moraad's rescue party. Chapter 2.
- ناشلیل، آسماری — origin of men who kill a guard with thrown stones during the rescue. Chapter 2.
- سِند — Raju's homeland, described only through his own remarks (canals, bridges, yearly water-councils without a king). Chapter 3.
- لَهْسون — village from which Halazh brings a coded scroll describing a water/date-division council. Chapter 3.
- شوش (Shush) — referenced repeatedly as a major city/seat with priests, a court, and a temple where Halazh once shouted his famous line; also referenced as a place from which refugees/travelers come and go. First: Chapter 3.
- جوبَجِی — mentioned alongside Shush as another seat of "شاهزادهها" that the army commander defects toward. Chapter 4, 9.
- دِزپارت، سوسن — villages that hear news of Nika's escape before Hani's riders arrive. Chapter 4.
- مَرْغا، سادات، شَیْوَند، غرِیویَل، مَوزَرْم — ruined regions Kuru and Shoterouro pass through while searching for Nika. Chapter 5.
- باجول، گَوِیْلِه، گَرْمِت، اَوْخیگون، اَوِنْد، موزَرْم، اَرْدَل، کُرِیْ — named passes/valleys in the search for Nika. Chapter 5.
- دِهْمورْد، اِشکفتجاموشی — location of Nika's year-earlier betrayal of refugees (per her deathbed confession). Chapter 7.
- تَرْکَوو، باغْاَنار، نوتَرْکی، صیدون، مالآقا — villages named in the unpaid-tax tablets shown to Hani. Chapter 9.
- تنَوش، زَراس، بلوطَک — villages reported to have defied tax collectors / redistributed grain / lowered Tirutir's flag during the "silence of the order" period. Chapter 9.
- نیرآوا — gardens visible from the palace window. Chapter 9.
- خونگ کمالوند، اَلیوند، بَردگوری جَنگه، کبنک، هیدالو، سیماش، پارسوماش — settlements whose people converge at Khong-e-Azhdar. Chapter 10.
- هُالگون — home village of a woman who proposes Eshkaft-e-Salman as the next meeting site. Chapter 11.
- سرگچ، درهباریک، پیون، موردغفار، جهجه — villages whose people travel to Eshkaft-e-Salman. Chapter 12.
- رودزرد، میْداوود — origin of upstream water-rights holders present at Eshkaft-e-Salman. Chapter 12.
- باغملک، صیدون، بارونگرد، قلعهتل — origins of some of the vote-bricks brought to Eshkaft-e-Salman. Chapter 12.
- َپرْسیال، کلدوزخ — home villages of three girls tending the knot-counting rope. Chapter 12.
- بَردْگَپی، سرقنات — origins of two men in a recorded exchange of views. Chapter 12.
- قلعهسَرد، سَرصحراخواجهوَر — men who discuss rumored court news. Chapter 12.
- آلونی، جونِقون، خانمیرزا (زنِ خانمیرزایی)، بروجن، دهکُرد — origin-villages of visiting mountain guests at the end of Chapter 12.
- دهْلی، کلْنرگس — places Kourash reports traveling from/through. Chapter 9.
- زردکوه، گَهْره — mountains referenced as part of the visiting mountain-people's home landscape. Chapter 12.
- مُنج، لُردِگون، سرخون، گندمکار، برهمُرده، دوپُلون، باجگیران، ناغون، اَرْدَل، بلداجی، گندمان، جونقان — destinations mapped by Kourash for the final caravan's outreach route. Chapter 12.
- رمهچر — home village of a woman present at Kuru's death. Chapter 13.
- هَلْتَمتی — a name/subject an elder from Kohbad teaches children about at Eshkaft-e-Salman; not otherwise defined in the text. Chapter 12. (Likely referring to the historical region/kingdom of Hatamti/Elam, but the text itself gives no gloss — UNKNOWN beyond the bare name.)
