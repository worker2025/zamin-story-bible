# Object Sheet: Chang

## Owner
Nika (original owner); passed by dying instruction to Manzat, co-custodianship with Ani; publicly played by an unnamed girl at Eshkaft-e-Salman thereafter. (Source: V05_Objects.md, V02_Characters.md)

## Material
UNKNOWN — described only as a wooden stringed instrument played by hand ("سیمها" / strings); precise construction (size, string count, wood type) UNKNOWN. (Source: V10_VisualDesign.md)

## Appearances
- Chapter 1 — Kolfarah: played for Manzat; played in the Narsina temple, prompting Nika's arrest. (V05_Objects.md)
- Chapter 2 — returned to Nika by Manzat via the young soldier. (V05_Objects.md, V03_Events.md E11)
- Chapter 7 — dropped when Nika is first struck; Nika's dying instruction to give it to Manzat; secretly played once by Manzat at night. (V05_Objects.md)
- Chapter 9 — first public playing after Nika's death, by Ani, unprompted. (V05_Objects.md)
- Chapter 10 — Khong-e-Azhdar: wind moves the strings unassisted before anyone touches them. (V05_Objects.md, V03_Events.md E51)
- Chapter 12 — Eshkaft-e-Salman: an unnamed teenage girl plays it for the first time after Nika; renamed in common speech from "چنگِ نیکا" to "چنگِ میدان." (V05_Objects.md, V03_Events.md E70)

## Events
E11, E28, E29, E39 (rebuilding window), E51, E70. (Source: V03_Events.md)

## Meaning (only as explicitly stated in Story Bible)
- Nika's own framing: "آواز توی چوب زندگی نمیکند... شاید توی آدمها." (V05_Objects.md)
- Nika's dying instruction: "هر وقت قایقش راهش را گم کرد، چنگ بزند. صدا... راه را پیدا میکند." (V05_Objects.md)
- After the girl's playing at Eshkaft-e-Salman, it is explicitly renamed by the community: "دیگر هیچکس نگفت چنگِ نیکاست... میگفتند: چنگِ میدان است." (V05_Objects.md)
