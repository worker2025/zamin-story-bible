# Visual Bible: Costumes

## Stated Costume Details (by character)

- Nika (captivity): torn, dusty shirt. (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Hani: crown/tāj; Appendix relief shows a round Elamite cap ("کاله گرد ایالمی"). (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Shoterouro: disguise ("لباس مُبدل") in the Ayapir bazaar — specifics UNKNOWN. (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Kuru: sword at belt over a laborer's frame; leather footwear split at the side; at death, an old cotton cloth/shirt ("چلوار کهنه"). (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Tela: heavily patched clothing. (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Hohin: simple dress with fine sleeve-fabric and beads/silver pin (Golshor); without armor or helmet at the ceasefire. (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Hotran: clean but patched clothing, salt-and-pepper beard. (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Nisiker: "همیشه لباسش مرتب بود" (always neat/orderly clothing). (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Halazh: trail-dust on his robe/cloak (رداء); a cloak/khirqa thrown to the ground at arrest. (V10_VisualDesign.md)

## General Costume/Material Notes
- Leather containers distinguished by hide type: خیگ (with wool/fleece) vs. مَشک (without wool). (V05_Objects.md, V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Soldiers' gear: spears (نیزه), shields (سپر), swords (شمشیر), helmets (کالهخود), armor (زره). (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Villagers/rebels armed only with tools during the Shileyti raid: spades (بیل), sickles (داس), adzes/chisels (تیشه), wooden clubs (گرزهای چوبی), shepherd's poles (نیزههای چوپانی). (V10_VisualDesign.md)
- Khong-e-Azhdar attackers ("چماقدارها"): wear bone necklaces marked with the emblems of Tirutir and Nahunte. (V10_VisualDesign.md)
