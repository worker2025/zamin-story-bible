# Visual Bible: Visual Rules

These rules are derived strictly from what the Story Bible documents as
present (or explicitly absent) in the manuscript's world. They are
restrictions, not creative additions.

## Rule 1 — No detail beyond documented canon
Any visual element not explicitly present in a Vxx Story Bible file (color,
architecture, costume, prop, lighting) must not be treated as canon. It may
be used as a production placeholder only if clearly marked "non-canonical /
production choice, not sourced from Story Bible."

## Rule 2 — Weapon/tool materials are unspecified
The Story Bible does not specify metal types for swords, sickles, or spear-
heads. Any depiction choice here is a non-canonical production decision.

## Rule 3 — Character appearances are largely unstated
Most named characters (see V10_VisualDesign.md) have no stated face, hair
color, height, or build. Any visual design for these characters is a
production invention outside the Story Bible and must be flagged as such
in any AI generation or illustration brief.

## Rule 4 — Real/historical sites vs. literary sites
Per V01_World.md, only Ayapir (=Izeh), Kolfarah, Khong-e-Azhdar, and
Eshkaft-e-Salman are stated to be real, historically attested locations.
Other locations (Kohbad, Golshor, Shileyti, Zapol-e-Simi) are not stated to
correspond to any specific real site, and no real-world visual reference is
canon for them per the Story Bible.

## Rule 5 — Deity/pantheon depictions
Only the deity names and epithets listed in V09_Religion.md are canon.
Iconography (appearance of gods, symbols) beyond the falcon image described
on Chishpish's hide (V02_Characters.md, V09_Religion.md) is UNKNOWN and not
canon.

## Rule 6 — Relief/epigraphic content is Appendix-sourced
Visual descriptions of the stone reliefs at Kolfarah, Khong-e-Azhdar, and
Eshkaft-e-Salman must be sourced only from the Appendix data reproduced in
V04_Locations.md and V10_VisualDesign.md — not freely designed.

## Validation
- No invented information
- Derived only from Story Bible
- UNKNOWN preserved
- Canon preserved
